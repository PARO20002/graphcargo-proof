1. The Problem

The global air cargo market moves $120B annually across 35,000+ active lanes. Pricing decisions — when to raise rates, when to secure capacity, when to shift corridors — are made on gut feeling, lagging benchmarks, or reactive spreadsheets. No system connects macro-economic cause to route-level logistics effect through a causal, explainable model.

COVID-19 tripled air cargo rates in 3 weeks with zero warning. The 2024 Red Sea crisis shifted sea freight to air overnight. China-US tariff escalation reshuffles corridors within weeks. The industry has data — it lacks intelligence.

1.1 Our Thesis

Macro-economic signals predict air cargo yield movements 4–6 weeks in advance. A causal knowledge graph can model and explain the propagation pathways from macro shock to route-level impact. This is provable with classical statistics (Pearson correlation, Granger causality) — not generative AI or LLM training.

3.3 The Causal Chain — From Signal to Yield

Every yield movement has a causal chain. The graph models it as a traversable path:

Example (proven): Copper price rises (+9.1%) → [:DRIVES] → BAI80 PVG rises (+28% in 5 weeks) → [:CONFIRMS] → WFC Yield on Asia→NAM rises (+29.6%). Lead time: 4–5 weeks. Pearson r = 0.857. Validated on 528 AWBs/week.

With TAC Space (Spire ADS-B), we add a physical confirmation layer: scheduled flights (OAG) vs. actual flights (Spire). Extra freighters outside the schedule = physical proof of demand surge. We discovered LH8426 (B777-200F, FRA→PVG) — not in OAG schedule — during the BAI80 spike.

5.2 Spot vs. Contract — Two Decision Logics

Spot and contract react to the same signals but with different speed, direction, and magnitude. The system generates separate forecasts for each:

Spot Forecast: FAX rate, TAC BAI Spot, weather disruption, cancellation rate, capacity tightness. Reacts fast, high volatility, 1–4 week horizon.

Contract Forecast: TAC Freight, WACD yield, PMI (4–8W lag), SCFI (2–4W), capacity trend, commodity mix shift. Reacts slow, low volatility, 4–12 week horizon.
