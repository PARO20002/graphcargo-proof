About GraphCargo

GraphCargo AI is an airfreight market intelligence platform currently in formation. We integrate 15+ data sources into a causal knowledge graph that serves airlines, freight forwarders, and GSAs with personalised market intelligence.

Target UsersAirlines (capacity management & yield optimisation), Forwarders (procurement timing), GSAs (portfolio optimisation & allotment negotiation)
TechnologySupabase PostgreSQL, Neo4j Graph Database, automated Edge Function pipelines
StatusPOC complete. Proven correlations on test data. 430,000+ AWBs processed, 15 data sources automated. First causal clusters identified in knowledge graph.

Key finding: With only 58 signals and limited test data, we identified 443 predictable lanes (Pearson r ≥ 0.7) and 130 lanes with r ≥ 0.95. Three corridor case studies proved that macro signals lead air cargo yield movements by 4-6 weeks. However, these correlations are not yet statistically validated as causal. This is why we need HPC.

Compute Workloads

We have identified five computational blocks that exceed what our cloud database (Supabase PostgreSQL) can handle. All workloads are classical statistics and graph analytics. No LLMs or generative AI.

BlockDescriptionScaleEst. GPU-h
1. Correlation MatrixEvery signal x every lane-commodity x every lag window (1-52 weeks) x rolling time windows~270 billion Pearson computations~2,000
2. Granger CausalityVAR model estimation, F-test, and bootstrap confidence intervals for every significant pair~27 million pairs x 100 bootstrap iterations~7,500
3. Flight Delta AnalysisOAG scheduled vs. Spire actual: matching, delta computation, correlation with market indices~55 million flight matches~200
4. Graph Data ScienceNode2Vec embeddings, community detection (Louvain), PageRank, link prediction on full knowledge graph12.5M nodes, 42M edges~500
5. ValidationSeasonal decomposition (STL), rolling-window out-of-sample backtesting across all corridors50M decompositions, 5.2M model fits~1,000
Total estimated compute requirement~11,200 GPU-h

Why cloud infrastructure is insufficient: Our Supabase PostgreSQL handles the live data pipeline and daily ingestion well. However, computing 270 billion correlations, running 2.7 billion Granger model fits, and executing Graph Data Science on 42 million edges requires dedicated HPC infrastructure with GPU acceleration and large shared memory. This is exactly what MeluXina provides.

Why MeluXina

Data sovereigntyOur data includes customer AWBs and licensed commercial datasets (WACD, OAG). European-hosted, Tier IV, ISO 27001, GDPR-compliant infrastructure is a requirement for our data partners and customers.
Architecture fitOur workload combines GPU-parallel econometrics (correlation, Granger) with memory-intensive graph analytics (Neo4j GDS). MeluXina's modular design with separate GPU and large-memory nodes maps directly to these two needs.
Expert supportAs a startup with limited HPC experience, the INITIATE programme's technical support (24 L1/L2 tickets) is important for optimising our workloads on the supercomputing platform.
Growth pathINITIATE provides the POC environment. Cashback 80% provides the commercial scaling path. This continuity avoids platform migration after the proof phase.
