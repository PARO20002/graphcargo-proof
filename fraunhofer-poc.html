1. Research Context

Global air cargo supply chains are structurally vulnerable to disruption. COVID-19 collapsed passenger belly capacity overnight. The 2024 Red Sea crisis rerouted sea freight to air. China-US tariff escalation reshuffles corridors within weeks. Yet the industry lacks quantitative early warning systems that connect macro-economic cause to logistics effect.

1.1 The Research Gap

Existing air cargo forecasting relies on historical demand extrapolation or expert judgement. No commercially available system connects macro-economic leading indicators (commodity prices, supply chain pressure indices, trade flows) to route-level yield and capacity outcomes through a causal, explainable model.

Our hypothesis: Macro-economic signals contain sufficient information to causally predict air cargo yield movements 4-6 weeks in advance. A knowledge graph can model and explain the propagation pathways from macro shock to route-level impact. This methodology is transferable from air freight to sea freight and multimodal logistics.

1.2 Three Research Questions

1
Causal prediction: Can macro-economic signals (GSCPI, commodity prices, bilateral trade flows) causally predict — not merely correlate with — capacity shortages and yield shifts in air cargo, validated through Granger causality and out-of-sample backtesting?
2
Explainable propagation: Can a causal knowledge graph (Neo4j Graph Data Science) model the propagation of disruption signals through the air freight network — from macro-economic trigger to market index confirmation to physical capacity change to route-level outcome?
3
Cross-modal transferability: Is the methodology applicable to sea freight by substituting air-specific data sources (BAI indices, ADS-B flight data) with maritime equivalents (BDI/SCFI indices, AIS vessel tracking)?

2.3 What the PoC Proves — and What It Does Not

Proven: Macro signals predict air cargo yields across corridors (r up to 0.89, 4-6W lead). Knowledge graph models causal chains. 430K+ real AWBs validate the approach.

Not yet proven: Causal direction (Granger), seasonal independence (STL), out-of-sample accuracy (backtest), full-scale graph analytics (HPC needed), sea freight transferability.

4.1 Funding Status and Data Access

Pre-funding. GraphCargo AI is self-funded. MeluXina INITIATE provides compute without cost. A Horizon Europe grant would fund the full 24-month programme including Fraunhofer and data licensing (TAC Index, OAG).

For research, we can provide Fraunhofer with anonymised correlation data (163K pairs), signal time series (58 signals), and the Neo4j knowledge graph immediately. Aggregated yield data and full AWB access require NDA and WFC consent.
